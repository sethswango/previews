Getting found on Google — quick wins we spotted

For a future round — nothing here is in this release

The goal: when someone in Springfield searches “marketing agency” or “branding agency,” Kidsister should be one of the names they see. Right now you’re not — Revel, Campaignium, Hook Creative, and Fated Studio are. Fated even leads with “women-led branding studio.” That’s your line.

What this round already fixed: every page now has a clear name Google can read and list, and we removed a leftover setting that told Google to skip new case-study pages entirely. That doesn’t jump rankings — it means nothing on the site is invisible or mislabeled anymore. Ground floor, done.

The next three, in payoff order:

  • Google Business Profile — the free listing that puts you in the map box beside those search results, showing the reviews you already earn. Biggest single win, costs nothing but setup.
  • Say “Springfield” where it counts — the site barely uses the words people actually type into Google. A few natural mentions give those searches something to match.
  • Make the contact page load fast — slow pages get marked down by Google and abandoned by people.

Happy to scope this as its own small round.
